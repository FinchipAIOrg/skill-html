01 / Fit

For consistency work, not random variations.

The studio treats your character as a small design system. Every new image is checked against the same visual contract.

Use it for

  • Original mascots and story characters
  • Expression, pose and turnaround sheets
  • Repeatable campaign or episode artwork
  • A clean handoff pack for future generations

Do not use it for

  • Identifying or copying an unknown artist's style
  • Unauthorized characters, brands or likenesses
  • Pixel-perfect 3D production turnarounds
  • Hidden details that cannot be responsibly inferred

03 / Lock

Write down what must survive.

A character lock separates non-negotiable identity from details that can vary safely.

Face & identifying marks
Outfit construction

Miko's non-negotiables

Left-ear notch. Exactly two coat toggles. Teal strap from character-left shoulder to right-hip satchel. Compact proportions. Warm paper-textured editorial finish.

04 / Prompt

Ask for a pack, not “more images.”

Attach your original reference and name the identity anchors you already know.

Use $one-character-studio to turn this original character reference into a starter asset pack.

Preserve: [identifying mark], [outfit construction], [prop placement], [role colors], and [rendering style].

Create: a turnaround, expression sheet, pose sheet, and hero portrait. Reject any candidate that drifts from the character lock. Package the final images, prompts, reviews, contact sheet, and validation report.

Tip: if a left/right detail matters, describe it from the character's point of view—not the viewer's.
