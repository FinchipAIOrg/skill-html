Repurposing without evidence drift.

Campaign Atomizer is for teams that need more useful content from one source without turning a small observation into a universal claim—or a single post into five near-duplicates.

Strong fit

  • Founder essays and thought leadership
  • Product launches and release briefs
  • Case studies with approved proof points
  • Podcast, webinar, and video transcripts
  • One-week, quick, launch, and evergreen packages

Not the right tool

  • Writing unsupported content from scratch
  • Copying a third party's protected work
  • Promising reach, leads, virality, or conversion
  • Producing finished images or videos
  • Publishing without explicit authorization

Evidence first. Copy second.

The workflow deliberately separates extraction, channel strategy, drafting, packaging, and validation.

01 · LEDGER

Atomize the source

Extract claims, numbers, quotes, stories, frameworks, opinions, and restrictions with stable IDs.

02 · ANGLES

Assign distinct jobs

Give every asset a role, audience promise, evidence selection, structure, and CTA.

03 · ADAPT

Write for the channel

Change register and format while preserving the underlying voice and source meaning.

04 · PACKAGE

Write real files

Create the manifest, content files, schedule, source ledger, angle map, and campaign plan.

05 · VERIFY

Fail unsafe copy

Check references, quotes, companion caveats, review flags, file parity, limits, and duplication.

Give it a source—and the boundaries around it.

Use pasted text or an accessible local file. A URL works only when the running agent has browsing available.

REQUIRED

Source content

Article, newsletter, case study, transcript, or launch brief plus rights status: owned, licensed, permission, or unknown.

REQUIRED

Audience + channels

Name the intended reader and at least one target platform. The Skill will not invent a performance ranking.

OPTIONAL

Voice capsule

Authorized samples, voice adjectives, sentence tendencies, CTA preference, and forbidden phrases.

OPTIONAL

Campaign constraints

Objective, mode, asset count, dates, approval-dependent claims, cadence, and visual requirements.

A prompt with enough context to preserve meaning.

If rights are unknown or the source is inaccessible, the Skill stops instead of filling the gap with plausible copy.

REVIEW

Human approval

Truth, rights, voice, platform fit, and current policies remain editorial gates.

Dependencies

The deterministic validator requires Python 3 and only its standard library. Browsing, visual creation, scheduling, and publishing are optional capabilities supplied by the running agent—not hidden dependencies.

Safety boundary

A passing report proves structural traceability, not real-world truth, ownership, current platform rules, or likely performance. Regulated, time-sensitive, comparative, confidential, and customer-specific atoms require human review.
