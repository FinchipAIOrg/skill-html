Decision Notes

Core value

Adds disagreement and readability to stock research by showing what different schools care about.

Usage reminder

Simulated comments are not real-person opinions and must be read with the source data.

Must-test samples

Large-cap compounder, small-cap theme stock, and high-hype weak-financial stock.

Failure signal

Every reviewer says the same generic line, or hot-money reviewers force a trade outside their range.

Standard areaThis benchmark now checksReject or watch signals
GateThe skill must have base stock context, dimensions, or raw analysis data before simulating a panel. The investor voices are personas, not real-time endorsements from named investors.Role-play without data, real-person impersonation, or advice-like consensus fails the gate.
Weighted scoreScore weighs persona coverage, field completeness, vote aggregation, disagreement quality, hot-money range checks, structured JSON/HTML usability, run cost, and safety wording.A colorful debate with no shared evidence base should score poorly.
Three-run stabilityRepeat one stock panel three times. Vote distribution may vary slightly, but evidence references, risk themes, and persona logic should remain recognizable.Personas changing criteria or inventing new base facts across runs is a failure signal.
Agent outputDownstream agents should receive panel votes, reason codes, persona-specific objections, consensus strength, and data gaps in a structured shape.Paragraph-only panel summaries are not enough for automated comparison.
Regression setKeep large-cap compounder, small-cap turnaround, and high-hype concept-stock cases as the regression baseline.Any panel that removes uncertainty or converts majority vote into a trade command should be blocked.
